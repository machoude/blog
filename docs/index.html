What you’ll find here

Data-Driven Insights — analytics on whether a player fits a club’s tactical and statistical profile, based on 4 seasons of per-90 data across 25+ leagues.

Transfer Grading — every transfer card shows a player’s percentile ranking in the target league, a projection tier, strengths and risks per metric, and an empirical success probability derived from the backtest.

Backtested Projections — the model is validated against real 25/26 outcomes. The probabilities you see are not assumptions — they are frequencies from historical transfers.

How to read the cards

Every transfer card on DataScouteR is built from the same set of rules. Understanding them makes every number meaningful.

Metrics are position-specific

Each metric is calculated and ranked only against players who play the same position. A Striker’s Scoring percentile is compared to other Strikers — not to Centre Backs or Goalkeepers. This matters because positions have completely different statistical profiles. A 72% Scoring for a Central Midfielder means something very different from a 72% Scoring for a Forward. The comparison is always apples to apples.

The six positions modelled are: Goalkeeper · Centre Back · Wing Back · Central Midfielder · Winger · Forward. Metrics are selected per position to reflect what actually matters at each role.

Percentiles are based on the most recent season only

The percentile ranking for a player uses only their most recent season’s statistics — not a career average. A player who was elite two seasons ago but declined in 25/26 is ranked on their 25/26 form. This is intentional: transfers are decisions about a player’s current profile, not their historical peak.

The reference distribution used for comparison spans all four seasons (22/23 to 25/26) in the target league, giving a robust baseline. But the player’s input is always their latest season.

What a percentile actually means

How to read a percentile number
72% This player outperforms 72 out of every 100 players at the same position in the target league on this metric. They are in the top 28%.
50% This player is exactly at the league median for this metric. Half the league is above them, half below.
33% This player is below the median. Two thirds of their positional peers in the league post higher numbers on this metric.

How metrics are scored

Each metric starts as a raw count (e.g. long balls per game). It is then:

  1. Normalised to per-90 minutes — removing playing time bias
  2. Scaled globally to 1–20 across all leagues and seasons on a single scale
  3. Multiplied by an Opta difficulty coefficient (ENG1 = 1.0, GRE1 = 0.75, etc.) — baking league difficulty directly into the score

This means a score of 14 in GRE1 and a score of 14 in ENG1 do not represent the same output — the Premier League score is unweighted, the Greek Super League score has been deflated to reflect that the same raw output is harder to achieve in a tougher league. Cross-league comparisons are therefore valid without any further adjustment.

The three colour bands on every card

✦ STRENGTH ≥ 60% The player clearly outperforms the majority of positional peers at this level. A genuine asset that a club can rely on.
◈ NEUTRAL 40–59% Around the league median. Competent but not a differentiator. These metrics won't win or lose a transfer argument.
▼ RISK < 40% Below the league median. The player will likely be exposed here relative to peers. How serious this is depends on how important the metric is to the specific club's system.

What the asterisk (*) on each card means

Every transfer card carries a footnote marked with *. It states three things that are easy to forget when reading a number:

  • The comparison group — which position and which league the percentile is drawn from
  • The data window — that the player’s scores are from their most recent season only, not a career average
  • What the model cannot see — injury history, tactical system fit, psychological adaptation, and contract terms are outside the scope of this analysis

The asterisk is not a disclaimer to ignore. It is a reminder that data answers how does this player rank statistically — a club’s scouts and coaching staff answer does this player fit our specific system.
